What foods should I avoid during a flare-up?

During a flare-up, it's best to avoid foods that can irritate your digestive system. Based on your tracked data and general recommendations, you should avoid:

  • High-fiber foods (raw vegetables, nuts, seeds)
  • Dairy products (especially if lactose intolerant)
  • Spicy foods
  • Fatty or fried foods
  • Caffeine and alcohol
  • Wheat products (based on your personal triggers)

Instead, focus on gentle, easily digestible foods like bananas, white rice, and well-cooked, skinless chicken.

Are there any supplements that could help with my fatigue?

Fatigue in Crohn's disease can be related to several factors including inflammation, anemia, or nutrient deficiencies. Based on your current supplement regimen, you might consider:

  • Iron supplements (if you have anemia)
  • Vitamin B12 (often poorly absorbed in Crohn's)
  • Vitamin D (you're already taking this, which is good)
  • CoQ10 (may help with energy production)
  • Magnesium (you missed your dose yesterday)

Always consult with your healthcare provider before starting any new supplements, as they may interact with medications or not be suitable for your specific situation.
